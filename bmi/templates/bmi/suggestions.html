How to Increase Your BMI

If your BMI is on the low side, you may be underweight. Of course, there could be various reasons why, so it’s best to consult with your doctor before making any major dietary or lifestyle changes.

Here are some healthy ways to increase your BMI:

Add healthy calories:

The most effective way to gain weight and consequently increase your BMI is to consume more calories than your body needs. Healthline suggests increasing by 300-500 calories daily above your maintenance level for steady weight gain. Incorporating nutrient-dense foods such as avocados, nuts, seeds, and eggs can contribute significantly to your daily calorie intake.

Eat More Frequently

Consider spreading out your food intake by having five to six smaller meals throughout the day instead of sticking to the traditional three larger ones. This approach can make increasing your calorie intake feel more manageable and less overwhelming.

Build Muscle

Incorporating strength training into your routine effectively contributes to weight gain by adding muscle mass instead of just fat. Working with a trainer initially to ensure you're performing exercises correctly can be beneficial.

{% elif bmi_classification == "Healthy" %}

Maintain a Healthy Weight

Being in the normal weight range is a good place to be. Here are some tips to help maintain your current weight:

Regular Exercise

Engage in regular physical activity to keep fit. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous activity each week, combined with muscle-strengthening exercises.

Balanced Diet

Maintain a balanced diet with a variety of nutrients. Incorporate plenty of fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains into your meals.

{% elif bmi_classification == "Overweight" %}

How to Reduce Your BMI

Here’s some advice for losing weight if your BMI is high:

Reduce your Daily Calorie Intake

EConsider reducing your daily intake by around 600 calories to lose weight safely. Use a food diary or online tracker to monitor your calories and make balanced food choices.

Move more

Increase your physical activity through regular cardio workouts like HIIT, spinning, or walking. Small daily changes, like taking the stairs, can also contribute to weight loss.

{% elif bmi_classification == "Obesity" %}

How to Manage Obesity

Addressing obesity requires a comprehensive approach. Here are some strategies:

Seek guidance from a healthcare provider

Work with a healthcare professional to create a personalized weight loss plan and address any underlying health conditions.

Focus on a healthy diet

Prioritize a balanced diet with portion control. Reduce intake of high-calorie, low-nutrient foods and increase consumption of vegetables and lean proteins.

Regular Exercise

Engage in regular physical activity to keep fit. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous activity each week, combined with muscle-strengthening exercises.
